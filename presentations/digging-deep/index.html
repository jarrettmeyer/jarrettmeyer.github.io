In ancient Sumerian (40th c. BCE), Inanna, queen of heaven, tries to overthrow her sister, Ereshkigal, the queen of the underworld. Inanna fails and dies, staying forever trapped in the underworld.

Eventually, a deal is struck and Inanna spends half the year in the underworld and half the year in heaven.

This creates the seasons.

In ancient Greek, Persephone, daughter of Zeus and Demeter, is stolen by Hades.

Eventually, a deal is struck and Persephone spends half the year in the underworld and half the year on Mount Olympus.

This creates the seasons.

In ancient Egypt, the god Set murders his brother Osiris. His body is put into a coffin and is cast into the Nile.

Osiris' wife, Isis, finds the body, embalms it, and gives it a proper burial. Osiris is resurrected and becomes the deity of the underworld.

His resurrection was such an event that every year the Nile floods, bringing new life and the ability to grow crops to the Egyptians.
