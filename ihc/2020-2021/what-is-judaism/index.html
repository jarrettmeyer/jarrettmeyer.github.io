The term "Orthodox Jews" first appears in Germany in 1795, and was used to describe those Jews opposed to integration with society. Prior to this time, Jews were largely able to keep to themselves in their own communities.

"Orthodox Jews" is used to define the cultural split between Jews open to the Enlightment and Jews opposed to integrating with society.
